Analyzing the effects of colonialism through the lenses of databases

Our database project aims to examine the lasting impact of colonialism on Africa, using relational, graph and spatial databases. Me and my partner explored major conflicts in Africa since decolonization and trace their roots to events that occurred during the colonial era. Through this, we demonstrated how these conflicts have affected the continent's economy, resources, and geopolitical relations, both historically and in the present day.

Our project also compares the differences and similarities between relational and graph databases and evaluate their respective strengths and weaknesses in various scenarios. By providing insights into the suitability of each database type, we hope to contribute to the ongoing conversation about the best practices for data management and analysis.

Graph database (Neo4j)

Graph databases are designed to handle complex relationships between data entities, making them well-suited for scenarios where relationships are a key focus. Therefore, we used them to show different kinds of relationships between African countries, the conflicts,colonial powers, organizations and so on.

For example in this comprehensive graph representation, we present an intricate web of relationships that encapsulate the dynamic narrative of the First Congo War. Each node within the graph embodies a distinct entity, be it a Colony, Other Country, Organization, or the driving Causes behind the conflict. These entities come to life through carefully curated labels and properties, painting a vivid picture of the diverse actors involved in this historical event.

Our graph visualization isn't just informative; it's designed for exploration. Users can delve deeper into specific aspects of the conflict, expand nodes of interest, and interact with the graph to uncover hidden details. A clear title and description provide context, while an intuitive legend deciphers the graph's visual language.

Relational Database(MySQL)

Our relational database has 17 tables.By combining this tables we can get many valuable information like how many people died in a specific conflict,how the economy of that country looks during each conflict, how many conflicts happened in British colonies, how many conflicts were caused over resource control and so on. We used relational databases to show numerical data because relational databases excel at organizing numerical data in structured tables with predefined schemas. This structure allows for efficient storage and retrieval of numerical values, making them suitable for large datasets with well-defined data types.
